# Waste Pad Cleaning Guide

This guide is intentionally conservative. It explains the physical maintenance principle without pretending that every Epson model opens in exactly the same way.

## Before you start

- Turn the printer off.
- Unplug power and USB.
- Protect the table with absorbent paper or disposable cloth.
- Wear gloves.
- Keep paper towels nearby.

## What you are cleaning

Waste ink pads absorb ink from:

- head cleaning cycles
- priming cycles
- purge operations
- maintenance routines

Resetting the counter without cleaning or replacing these pads is unsafe.

## Typical cleaning workflow

1. Identify the waste ink pad area or maintenance absorber area.
2. Open only the service covers that are clearly intended to be opened.
3. Remove the absorbent pads or absorber assembly if the model allows it.
4. Blot excess ink first with paper towels.
5. Wash the pads with warm water until the water runs much clearer.
6. Let the pads dry completely before reinstalling them.
7. Clean any spilled ink inside the service area.
8. Reassemble the printer.
9. Only after the pads are dry and back in place, run the software reset.

## If the pads cannot be safely removed

- Do not force plastic clips or hidden screws.
- Replace the absorber assembly if a spare part is available.
- If removal is impossible, stop and look for the service manual or model-specific teardown before continuing.

## What "dry" means

The pads should not be:

- dripping
- visibly wet
- cold and saturated to the touch

If they are still wet, wait longer. Reinstalling wet pads can move ink into places where it should not go.

## After reassembly

- Power the printer on.
- Check for leaks before printing.
- Run the software counter reset.
- Print a small test page.

## Important warning

The script in this repository only clears the electronic lock/counters.
It does not perform physical maintenance for you.
